Kids of Colour

A YouTube platform for kids of colour to explore their experiences of race, identity and culture in modern Britain

Visit our channel

Why

We work to inform society on the diverse experiences belonging to young people of colour in Britain, humanizing them and challenging the narrative that makes them either one-dimensional or non-existent. We want to build a picture of how young people of colour are affected by race, identity and culture, encouraging others to deepen their understanding of a community whom many ‘assume’ they know.

Who

Kids of Colour is led by Roxy Legane, who has worked in the youth and charity sector since 2013. She set up Kids of Colour in February 2018, tired of society’s ways of representing young people of colour homogenously; rarely sharing their individual experiences, interests or views.

Get Involved

Contribute

If you are a young person of colour, aged 24 and under, we would love to hear from you. You do not have to have done this kind of thing before, we will help you explore what you want to share. All experiences are valuable, and your insights are important.
